Sliding adjustment of biased data

We designed this tool to give analysts a sense for the hidden variables that mediate relationships between variables in a dataset.

This particular graph allows the exploration of how the relationship between unemployment and city size (logged) is mediated by the educational makeup of a city.

Unemployment tends to be concentrated in large cities, which attract those from smaller places looking for jobs and experience larger business cycles. This suggests a positive slope to the relationship between unemployment.

Generally, since unemployment is negatively related to education (i.e. unemployment is lower in more highly educated places), and since the highly educated tend to live in larger cities, the relationship between unemployment and population is even stronger than it initially appears.

You can explore this relationship by increasing the weight on educational composition, and watching the effect on the slope.
